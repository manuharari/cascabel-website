01 — The Challenge

Always buying
at the peak.

The procurement department operated reactively: when material levels got low, they bought. No price modeling, no demand forecasting, no market visibility.

The result was predictable: frequent purchases during price surges, overstocking when large orders arrived, and tied-up capital that no one could accurately justify.

"We bought when we ran short, not when it was the right time. It's hard to compete on costs that way."

— Procurement Director
02 — The Solution

Mercado:
signals before the surge.

Mercado continuously monitors the prices of the plant's key materials—international reference markets, local suppliers, historical trends—and crosses them with real consumption and current inventory to produce concrete recommendations.

It's not a price dashboard. It's a system that tells the procurement team what to do: buy now, wait, or hedge with a fixed-price order—providing the reasoning behind every move.

It also models future material demand based on the production plan, so procurement and operations finally speak the same language. Implementation took approximately four months, including model calibration with the plant's purchase history.

03 — The Impact

Less inventory.
Better pricing.

The effective price paid for primary materials dropped by roughly 25% in the first year—not because suppliers changed, but because the plant started buying at better times.

Stagnant inventory fell by nearly 40%, freeing up working capital that previously sat idle in the warehouse. Procurement shifted from reacting to the market to anticipating it.

"For the first time, procurement and production are aligned. We know what we'll need before we actually need it."

— Operations Director
